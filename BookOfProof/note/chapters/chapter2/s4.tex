\subsection{Biconditional Statements}
\begin{definition}
    [Biconditional Statement]
    is a compound logic statement combining a conditional ($P \implies Q$) and its converse ($Q \implies P$).
    $$P \iff Q = (P \implies Q) \wedge (Q \implies P)$$
\end{definition}

$Q \implies P$ is called the converse of $P \implies Q$.

Here is the true-false table for biconditional statements. 
\[
\begin{array}{c|c|c}
p & q & p \leftrightarrow q \\
\hline
\text{T} & \text{T} & \text{T} \\
\text{T} & \text{F} & \text{F} \\
\text{F} & \text{T} & \text{F} \\
\text{F} & \text{F} & \text{T}
\end{array}
\]

The most precise english meaning for biconditional statement is: 
\begin{center}
    $P \iff Q$ \\
    $P$ is true if only if $Q$ is true. 
\end{center}
