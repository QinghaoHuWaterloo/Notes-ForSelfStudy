\subsection{And, Or, Not}
Let $P$ and $Q$ be statemetns.
Then $P \wedge Q$ stands for "P and Q";
$P \vee Q$ stands for "P or Q";
$P \implies Q$ stands for "P implies Q";
$P \iff Q$ stands for "P if Q";
$\neg P$ stands for "Not P". 

The truth of these statements depends on the truth of P and Q. It can be shown by a truth table:
\begin{center}
  \begin{tabular}{cccccccc}
    % \toprule
    $P$ & $Q$ &\quad &$P\wedge Q$ & $P\vee Q$ & $P\Rightarrow Q$ & $P\Leftrightarrow Q$ & $\neg P$ \\
    % \midrule
    T & T & & T & T & T & T & F\\
    T & F & & F & T & F & F & F\\
    F & T & & F & T & T & F & T\\
    F & F & & F & F & T & T & T\\
    % \bottomrule
  \end{tabular}
\end{center}
