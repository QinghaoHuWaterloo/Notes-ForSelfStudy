\subsection{Theorems}
\begin{definition}
    [Theorem]
    A theorem is a mathematical statement that is true and has been proved to be true. 
\end{definition}
Most of the theorems are conditional statements, which means most of theorems involved "If $\cdots$ then $\cdots$" statement. 

\begin{definition}
    [Proposition]
    A statement that is true but not as significant. 
\end{definition}
\begin{definition}
    [Lemma]
    A lemma is a theorem whose main purpose is to help prove another theorem. 
\end{definition}
\begin{definition}
    [Corollary]
    Corollary is a result that is an immediate consequence of a theorem or proposition. 
\end{definition}
