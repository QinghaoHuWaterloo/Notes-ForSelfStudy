\subsection{Direct Proof}
Direct proof is always used to prove conditional statements. 
For the sake of simplicity, let's prove a conditional statement of $P \Rightarrow Q$.

Just read the textbook, it is easier. 

\begin{proof}
    We rewrite all the series into 
    \[
        n! + a
    \]
    where $a \in \N, 2 \leq a \leq n$.
    Then, $n! + a = a\left( \frac{n!}{a} + a\right)$
\end{proof}
